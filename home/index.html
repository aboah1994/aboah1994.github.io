About Principal Investigator

Assistant Professor Aboah Armstrong is an Assistant Professor at North Dakota State University. He is an ingenious and resourceful Transportation Data Scientist with a proven track record of success in research and hands-on experience developing cutting-edge database solutions, statistical modeling, data products, and computer vision systems aimed at improving transportation system management and operations.

His broad interests lie in computer vision and machine learning. His research involves visual reasoning, vision and language, image generation, air taxis, naturalistic studies, and autonomous vehicles. He received his PhD from the University of Missouri-Columbia, where he was advised by Professor Yaw Adu-Gyamfi.

Research Focus Areas

Our research spans multiple domains, contributing to both theoretical understanding and practical applications.

Big Data Analytics In Transportation

Leveraging large-scale data and machine learning to uncover insights, predict trends, and support decision-making in transportation systems

Autonomous and Connected Vehicles

Exploring the development, deployment, and societal impacts of self-driving and networked vehicles

Digital Twins and Smart Cities

Utilizing digital replicas of physical assets and urban environments to optimize city planning, operations, and sustainability

Intelligent Transportation Systems

Integrating advanced technologies to create smarter, adaptive transportation networks

Pavement and Asset Management

Developing methods for monitoring, maintaining, and optimizing the lifecycle of roads, bridges, and other transportation assets

Transportation Safety

Roadyways accidents reduction using data analysis,simulation and safety interventions
